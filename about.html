The Beginning

World War one was initially started by a assassination on the Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria on the 28th of June 1914. After that countries would be bound by alliances to fight their neighbors. It was a long and bloody war for the europeans. By the end the death count would reach about 18 million dead. With the US joining to fight alongside the allied powers. The axis would realize their chances of winning the war were very slight. So on the 11th of November 1918 the German government surrendered. And the fighting was over at 11 am.

1914: On June 28, the Archduke Ferdinand, the heir to the throne of Austria-Hungary, is assassinated in Sarajevo. One month later, Austria-Hungary declares war on Serbia. Between August 1 and 6, Germany declares war on Russia and France; Great Britain then declares war on Germany; Austria-Hungary declares war on Russia; Serbia declares war on Germany; and the United States remains neutral. 1915: The Germans torpedo and sink the British ship Lusitania off the southern coast of Ireland. The ship was carrying almost 2,000 civilian passengers, and this event increased tension between America and Germany. 1916: The Battle of Verdun begins in February and is the longest and bloodiest battle of World War 1. 1917: The Zimmerman telegram is intercepted by the British in January, in which Germany tried to lure Mexico to their side by promising them U.S. territory. The U.S. officially enters the war in April.
