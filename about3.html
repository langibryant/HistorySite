After Effects of the War

As a result of the war, millions died, not only soldiers but the civilians. Civilian deaths were higher than the casualties of the fighting forces, the main causes of the death counts in the home countries of those who fought were starvation, disease, exposure, massacres and military encounters. In Germany, from 1918 to 1919 a food shortage broke out the because of the blockade was kept during the “peace negotiation in Paris”.


From mostly casualties and wounds caused mostly by artillery, small arms and then poison gas, about 57.6% of the armies deployed died. From the staggering 65,038810 mobilized forces about 37,468,904 was the casualty number.
